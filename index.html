    Welcome #

    About this website #

    This website is the result and documentation of my project “Hybrid Quantum-Classical machine learning” as part of the seminar “Recent Topics in Quantum computing” in Summer 2020.

    To the top on the right you can find the structure of the individual page, in the top left corner you can find the navigation between pages.

    The option was given to be graded by a written technical report or by submiting a code implementation. I chose the latter one and this the of my topic “Hybrid quantum classical machine learning”. My aim was to recreate the paper of Thomas Hubregtsen et al. 1

    ”What I cannot create, I do not understand“ - Richard Feynman

    While already struggling with complicated equation enough, I found the amount of quality tutorials quite lacking.

    Prerequisites #

    🔮 Machine Learning #

    While probably not 100% necessary to understand most of this Project, basic knowledge about Neural Networks and gradient-based optimization sure is helpful. I can recommend the Books by Bishop or Murphy

    🌐 Quantum Computing #

    To understand what these circuits do basic knowledge of quantum gates, general theory behind measurements and XXX are expected. Nielsen and Chuang is a good read and seems to be the standart literature. Furthermore, this videoseries by CMU is really great to get an intuition fast (and it is really entertaining, too!): Quantum Computation and Information at CMU
